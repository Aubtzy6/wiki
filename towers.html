  • Recruit

    Has been recruited to help out the battlefield! The starter tower of the game.

    Cost: Free

    The Recruit is a well-rounded and affordable starter tower, perfect for players beginning their journey in Retro Defense. Its versatility makes it suitable for most early-game situations, but it becomes less effective as stronger towers become available later in the game.

    Upgrades

    The top path offers excellent hidden detection and impressive range, making it a preferred choice for most situations. While its DPS is modest, its affordability and reliable hidden coverage make it a strong and versatile option.


    The bottom path boasts impressive DPS and an ability that temporarily boosts its damage output even further. However, its effectiveness is hindered by the "magazine" and "reload" mechanics, which create downtime where the tower can't attack, allowing units to slip through. Additionally, the lack of hidden detection limits its versatility, often leaving it overshadowed by the more reliable top path. While the ability helps mitigate the reload issue, it serves as only a temporary fix, making this path less desirable overall.

  • Sharpshooter

    Shoots enemies from a distance, dealing high damage. A good starter tower.

    Cost: 500

    This tower is a powerful and versatile option, boasting far-reaching attacks and innate hidden detection that make it a valuable asset in any loadout. Whether upgraded along the top or bottom path, it remains a reliable choice. However, its effectiveness comes with trade-offs, including a slow attack speed, placement restrictions limiting it to elevated areas of the map, and high costs for upgrades as the game progresses. Despite these challenges, its strengths shine, especially for new players looking for a dependable and impactful tower to anchor their strategy.

    Upgrades

    The top path transforms the tower into a hard-hitting powerhouse, granting significantly higher damage per shot and a unique passive ability to deal double damage to boss units. While this sounds impressive, its practical use is limited, as boss units only appear in the final wave (or two with the buffed waves modifier enabled). Additionally, the higher cost of this crosspath makes it a less appealing investment, especially when compared to other towers that offer more consistent value for boss damage throughout a match. Ultimately, this path struggles to justify its price tag and situational effectiveness.


    The bottom path enhances the tower with significantly increased range and attack speed, alongside a powerful ability that temporarily doubles its attack rate. This combination allows the tower to deliver more overall damage to targets compared to its top path, especially when its ability is used strategically. While it stands out as the more versatile option, this path also retains the capability to unleash a devastating burst of damage on high-priority threats, making it an adaptable and reliable choice for dealing with various challenges in a match.

  • Bomber

    After playing Brick Battles, Bomber has came to help defend against hordes of enemies! Deals splash damage.

    Cost: 750

    This early-game splash damage tower offers affordability and the potential to stun units based on its crosspath. However, it is widely regarded as the weakest tower in the game. The Paintballer outshines it in every niche, providing better DPS, hidden coverage, and overall utility. Its lack of reliability in damage output, inability to handle hidden units effectively, and dependence on full investment into an X-4 Bomber crosspath to make its stun ability marginally useful leave it without a defined role. As a result, this tower is rarely worth considering for any serious strategy.

    Upgrades

    Top path provides hidden detection along with respectable range and damage, making it a reasonable choice for early-game coverage. However, it falls short when compared to other towers in terms of hidden unit effectiveness. In fact, its hidden coverage is only better than that of the Heavy Gunner and Mortar, leaving it insufficient for handling more challenging hidden units as the game progresses. As hidden units become more tanky later in the match, a low-DPS AOE tower like this one simply cannot compete, making it a poor investment for sustained hidden unit defense.


    The bottom path offers the ability to stun enemy units with each attack and provides the higher DPS option for the tower. However, the stun effect is minimal at its X-3 upgrade, lasting only a brief quarter of a second. It's not until the X-4 upgrade that the stun duration becomes long enough to have a noticeable impact on gameplay. Despite this improvement, the investment required to make the stun effect viable is not justified, especially when compared to more cost-efficient and effective alternatives like the Paintballer, which outperforms this path in terms of overall value and utility.

  • Swordsman

    After many times practicing on SFOTH, Swordsman is ready to slash enemies. Deals area damage.

    Cost: 1000

    This tower boasts a compact range but compensates with exceptional DPS, rapid attack speed, and an affordable cost. Its reliability and effectiveness make it a standout choice, holding its value consistently from early to late game. Undeniably, it reigns as the best tower in the game, providing unparalleled utility and performance in any strategy.

    Upgrades

    The top path is the definitive choice for this tower, offering unmatched value and utility. It is highly affordable, granting Hidden detection and Stun protection at a minimal cost. At the 4-X upgrade, it unlocks an additional poison effect that deals massive damage over time, particularly devastating against tanky enemies, dealing hundreds to thousands of damage as a secondary effect to its primary attacks. Remarkably, a 4-X Swordsman can outshine even a Heavy Gunner in raw DPS against the final boss. This path is often considered a win condition for challenging runs with specific modifiers, providing exceptional performance at a cost that remains competitive with other high-damage towers.


    The bottom path focuses on boosting the Swordsman's DPS while introducing a burn effect to its attacks. However, its shortcomings overshadow its benefits. Lacking both Hidden detection and Stun protection, the tower is highly vulnerable to explosive units, as its short range ensures it will always be stunned when engaging them. While it theoretically deals higher DPS, this potential is rarely realized due to its inconsistency. Furthermore, the tankiest units in the game are camouflaged, making the lack of Hidden detection a critical flaw. Comparatively, the top path delivers far superior reliability, damage output, and versatility thanks to its poison effect and added utility, easily justifying its slightly higher cost. As a result, the bottom path is a poor investment and rarely worth considering in any strategic setup.

  • Paintballer

    After being neglected, Paintballer has come to Retro Defense ready to fight off hordes of enemies! Deals splash damage.

    Cost: 1500

    Level 5
    div

    This early splash damage tower sets itself apart with unparalleled support capabilities, making it an invaluable asset in any game. It excels at applying powerful crowd control effects, including the ability to slow enemy units or, at higher upgrades, completely remove their Hidden status. Its wide splash radius ensures these effects impact multiple enemy units with each attack, enhancing its utility across various scenarios. Despite its potent support role, this tower remains modestly priced, offering exceptional value for strengthening any tower composition on the map.

    Upgrades

    The top path elevates this tower into one of the best support options in the game. At its 3-X upgrade, it provides cost-effective Hidden detection, but its true strength shines at the 4-X upgrade, where it gains the unique ability to completely remove the Hidden status from any enemy it attacks. This transformative capability enables unparalleled synergy with every other tower, allowing players to allocate resources more freely into BP upgrades without worrying about Hidden units bypassing their defenses. Its synergy is especially impactful when paired with the Mortar, as the removal of Hidden status ensures all units in its splash radius can be targeted. This path's utility and cost-effectiveness solidify both it and the tower as top-tier choices for any strategy.


    The bottom path focuses on slowing down enemy units, reducing their movement speed as they traverse the track. While the ability’s value peaks at the cost-effective X-3 upgrade, which provides sufficient slow utility for most scenarios, the X-4 upgrade offers a moderate increase in both DPS and the slow effect’s duration and effectiveness. However, the improvement in the slow effect is minimal, making the X-4 investment harder to justify unless the added DPS is critical for your strategy. Overall, this path is a decent option for utility with a situational DPS boost at higher upgrades.

  • Coder

    Creates games and scripts to earn you money! Generates money.

    Cost: 2000

    This tower serves a purely economic role, generating income at set intervals during a game. While its concept has potential, its current implementation falls short. The game's duration is too brief for the tower to recoup the significant investment it demands, making it an inefficient choice. Additionally, the game can be completed just as easily without the need for its income-generating abilities, leaving little incentive to include it in a strategy. As it stands, this tower is more of a novelty than a practical option for serious gameplay.

    Upgrades

    The top path maximizes this tower's income-generating potential, offering the highest monetary return at a relatively affordable cost. Its efficiency in producing large amounts of income quickly makes it the premier choice for players looking to amass resources in the shortest time possible. This path is the go-to option for those prioritizing economic growth during gameplay.


    The bottom path offers a mix of income generation and utility, allowing the tower to regenerate health for the base and, at the X-4 upgrade, provide a 10% discount to all towers within its range. However, this path struggles to justify its steep investment. The 10% discount is underwhelming and limited in scope, especially when compared to the financial benefits of simply placing another top-path Coder. Additionally, the health regeneration feature, while helpful in theory, is often unnecessary if the resources required to upgrade to a X-4 CODER are instead used to strengthen defenses. Ultimately, this path fails to compete with the more efficient and impactful top path, making it a suboptimal choice.

  • Mortar

    Launches explosive shells with high splash radius and damage. Can ignite and stun enemies at max.

    Cost: 2500

    The Mortar is among the priciest towers in the game but compensates with powerful splash attacks and impressive range. Despite its strengths, it is hindered by several drawbacks, including placement restrictions limiting it to elevated areas, a slow attack speed, high cost, and an inability to detect Hidden units. While it excels at dealing massive area damage, its utility is heavily dependent on being paired with a 4-X Paintballer to address its lack of Hidden detection. Without this pairing, the Mortar struggles to justify its steep investment, making it a situational but impactful tower when used strategically.

    Upgrades

    The top path significantly enhances the tower, offering the highest range and damage along with a burn effect that increases in both effectiveness and duration as it is upgraded. However, this path comes with notable drawbacks, particularly its slow attack speed and high cost, making it the most expensive upgrade for the tower. It also ranks as the third most expensive upgrade in the game, with only the Heavy Gunner's upgrades surpassing it in price. While it delivers powerful damage and utility, the high cost and slow attack speed make it a challenging investment that requires careful consideration.


    The bottom path provides modest increases to damage and range, but its real strength lies in its upgrades to cooldown reduction and burst firing capabilities, allowing the tower to shoot three shots at a time in rapid succession. This burst mechanic acts as a valuable fix for the tower's slow attack speed, significantly improving its overall reliability. However, this improvement comes at the cost of some damage output, as the burst fire mechanics reduce the individual shot damage. Despite this trade-off, the bottom path proves to be a more consistent and dependable option for handling waves, especially when high attack speed is crucial.

  • Barracks

    Spawns units on the path, moving from our base to the entrance. Has 7 units to help defeat the infection!

    Cost: 3500

    This tower is one of the best in the game, known for its efficiency and cost-effectiveness. It spawns units that move along the track, stopping whenever an enemy unit enters its range and attacking it until the enemy is destroyed. Once there are no more enemies in range, the spawned units continue walking along the track. What sets this tower apart is its innate Hidden detection, due to its ram damage, making it invaluable for dealing with Hidden units throughout the game. With its strong utility and solid performance, it remains a reliable and affordable defense option for any strategy.

    Upgrades

    The top path enhances this tower by spawning stronger units and granting them the ability to detect Hidden enemy units, making it an excellent option for Hidden coverage. The 3-X upgrade is a great choice for cost-effective Hidden detection, providing reliable utility throughout the game. At the 4-X upgrade, the tower gains the ability to spawn an additional, more powerful unit. However, the extra unit spawns too slowly and in too few quantities to justify the high cost, making it less valuable when compared to other upgrades. While it provides some benefits, the top path can be a bit too slow and expensive for consistent effectiveness.


    The bottom path offers a more cost-effective alternative, focusing on increasing the tower's DPS. While the units spawned are not necessarily stronger than those from the top path, this path accelerates the spawn rate of every unit, allowing for faster coverage. Additionally, it introduces an extra unit with longer range, higher damage, and splash damage capabilities, adding significant value to the tower's offensive output. The X-4 upgrade of this path offers more substantial benefits compared to the top path's 4-X upgrade, making it the better choice for boosting DPS while maintaining a lower cost.

    Cost: 10000

    Level 20

    This tower boasts the highest DPS in the game, delivering immense damage that rivals its steep cost.     The bottom path has the cheapest upgrade for this tower, yet it remains the second most expensive upgrade in the game. Despite its high cost, it is unmatched in terms of DPS, making it an excellent choice for dealing raw damage in any situation, provided you have the resources to afford it. This path excels at delivering immense damage output, but its hefty price tag limits its use, requiring a substantial cash surplus to make it a worthwhile investment. For players looking to maximize DPS, this path is a top contender, though its cost may deter some.
